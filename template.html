Projects

Robot Design Studio Capstone - Winter/Spring 2022

My senior capstone project through robot design studio had teams of 6 students build a robot centered around haptics. Our goal was to create a display surface (a tablet) with a four degree-of-freedom robotic mechanism hidden underneath it. Users would couple through the tablet via magnets to produce haptic sensations tangent to the surface of the display.

4WD Powertrain System & Gearbox - 2020-2021

I designed and manufactured the powertrain system for the NU Baja's 2021 vehicle (Dory), converting to four-wheel drive after generations of two-wheel drive cars. This system consists of all components that transfer power from the engine to the wheels. Dory went on to place 6th in endurance and 8th overall at our national competition, NU Baja's best ever placement.

Front Suspension A-Arms

I designed and manufactured the double wishbone suspension components, ensuring compatibility with the rest of the suspension system. To complete the design, I used finite element analysis to simulate various crash scenarios, optimizing for weight while maintaining a 1.3 Factor of Safety.

National Science Foundation Research Experiences for Undergraduates - Additive Materials Fatigue Testing

I overhauled the LabVIEW control interface for a piezoelectric fatigue testing machine to create a more friendly user interface, improve efficiency by 10%, and implemented a method of data collection. With all of the code in place, I was able to begin conducting fatigue tests to gather data on microstructure and mechanical properties of samples created via additive manufacturing.
