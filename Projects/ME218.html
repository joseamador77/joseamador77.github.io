ME 218 - Smart Product Design Series

ME 218ABC gives a deep dive into programmable electromechanical systems using an embedded microprocessor (PIC32) and the C programming language. 218A covers circuit analysis/design, signal conditioning, SPI communcation, and hardware abstraction layers. 218B covers closed and open loop motor control(steppers/brushed DC), interrupts, timers, and PWM. 218C covers assembly language and communcation protocols.

ME218A - NintenPals

The goal of this project was to build an electromechanical widget using sensors, actuators, and event driven software. We created a game inspired by the hit 2005 video game Nintendogs for the nintendo DS. Our solution utilized SPI communcation, signal conditioning, analog inputs, and a servo.

ME218B Machine

The goal of this project was to designa nd build a machine to autonomously play a game. This 1v1 game consisted of 2 sides and 3 lanes each with a movable wall. The goal was to push each wall to your opponents side and the player with the most area on their side wins. My team built a wheeled mobile robot that utilzied IR signal conditioning, 3 ultra sonic sensors, and 4 IR reflectance sensors to autonomously navigate between lanes and push each wall the maximum distance. Our solution proved to be simple and robust allowing our team to claim victory in the class tournament.

T-Wrecks

The project was a change to apply all of the knowledge gained rom the 218 series with an emphasis on communcation. We were tasked with building a controller, a mini 218A porject, and a boat, a non- autonomous 218B project. The two would wirelessly communcate using a class defined protocol via XBee radios such that any controller can easily control any boat. The boats played a game of "sharks and minnows" and each was equipped with a balloon and a sharp weapon to pop opponents' balloons.
