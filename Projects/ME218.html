ME 218 - Smart Product Design Series

ME218ABC gives a deep dive into programmable electromechanical systems using an embedded microprocessor (PIC32) and the C programming language. 218A covers circuit analysis/design, signal conditioning, SPI communication, and hardware abstraction layers. 218B covers closed and open loop motor control(steppers/brushed DC), interrupts, timers, and PWM. 218C covers assembly language and communication protocols.

ME218A - NintenPals

The goal of this project was to build an electromechanical widget using sensors, actuators, and event-driven software. We created a game inspired by the hit 2005 video game Nintendogs for the Nintendo DS. Our solution utilized SPI communication, signal conditioning, analog inputs, and a servo.

ME218B Machine

This project was to autonomously play a 1v1 game. The goal of the game was to navigate between 3 lanes and push 3 sliding walls to the opponent's side, the player with the most area on their side at the end of time wins. My team built a wheeled mobile robot that utilized IR signal conditioning, 3 ultra-sonic sensors, 4 IR reflectance sensors, and hierarchical state machines to autonomously navigate between lanes and push each wall the maximum distance. Our solution proved to be simple and robust allowing our team to claim victory in the class tournament.

T-Wrecks

The project was a chance to apply all of the knowledge gained from the 218 series with an emphasis on communication. We were tasked with building a controller, a mini 218A project, and a boat, a non-autonomous 218B project. The two would wirelessly communicate using a class-defined protocol via XBee radios such that any controller can easily control any boat. The boats played a game of "sharks and minnows" and each was equipped with a balloon and a sharp weapon to pop opponents' balloons.
