FIRST Robotics Competition Team 670 - Sunnnyvale, CA

The FIRST Robotics competition (FRC) is a high school robotics competition that combines the excitement of sport with the rigors of science and technology. Each year brings a new challenge and teams have six weeks to design, prototype, build, and test 120lb robots to compete with other teams from across the world. I was a part of Team 670 - Homestead Robotics for 4 years throughout high school, eventually becoming the president of the team my senior year. As the president I led a team of ~40 students and organized competition logistics, coordinated team projects, and delegated tasks to the proper subteams. Homestead Robotics Website

Each year I designed and prototyped mechanisms for a 120lb robot to meet the annual design challenges. My junior year I created a ball shooter for wiffle balls and my senior year I created an elevator mechanism that could reach heights of up to 8 feet. Since each team only has 6 weeks to build a complete robot, I learned a lot about how to create quality, rapid prototypes. This competition ignited my passion for STEM, ultimately inspiring me to pursue a degree in mechanical engineering. Since I was in FRC as a high schooler, documentation is pretty sparse, but I did manage to find a couple of photos and videos. Enjoy!
